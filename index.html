Good Visualization 1:

Link to Visualization

Who is the audience?

The audience is people that are interested in both the origins of memes over the past decade as well as people who are interested in internet culture as a whole and how it has evolved from 2010-2022.

What message and questions does this visualization convey and answer?

This visualization answers the questions of which social platforms had the largest hand in where popular memes came from - the percentage of shares a platform had during a given year. It also gives viewers insight, to some extent, of how different social media platforms eb and flow in popularity during the twelve year span analyzed. We can see that certain social media platforms are more prevalent during certain time spans in terms of where popular memes originate from.

What data is encoded in the visualization?

Information on the percent of the shares of origination of memes in a twelve year time span from nine different social media platforms.

How does the visualization encode the data?

It encodes it in a line chart that uses color to distinguish which social media platform corresponds to which line tracing percentage.

What tasks do readers perform on the visualization?

Readers are able to compare the percentage of origination per year that each social media company had. They are also able to gain insight into the influence these platforms have had over the past decade and draw conclusions about the popularity of said platforms.

How are the five principles applied to this visualization?

  • Beautiful: It is aesthetically pleasing to look at and the colors in specific compliment one another nicely making it beautiful to viewers.
  • Functional: The graph is constructed in a way that it can be easily analyzed by viewers with the lines being easily distinguishable and the data being easy to understand and digest.
  • Insightful: It uses data - looking at where memes were shared from - that as readers we would not necessarily have access to, and certainly would not look at a sample this size without this graph being available.
  • Truthful: The visualization is truthful because it took a sample of between 700-1,400 memes and analyzed the percentage of meme origin shares it had across nine different platforms.
  • Enlightening: It is enlightening because as readers we can understand where memes are being shared from and how this relates to both the popularity of certain platforms over a given period of time and how platforms eb and flow in popularity.

Why is this visualization good

We believe that this visualization is good because it is not only aesthetically pleasing and conveys interesting and relevant information about the origin of popular memes, but because it is truthful and easy to understand for readers. The design itself is very clean and straightforward, which limits the need for over-analysis by readers and the possibility of misunderstanding the data. The use of color in the line chart makes it easy to identify which line corresponds to which company and track the overall progression of meme origins.

Good Visualization 2:

Link to Visualization

Who is the audience?

This graph is for people who are interested in politics and racial divides on voting. With the upcoming election, voting matters, so every American has the responsibility to vote. It is important to understand the demographics to heal the divide between two parties.

What message and questions does this visualization convey and answer?

This visualization wants to convey the voting situation of non-white votes during the 2018 midterm election. The message is interesting that there are a lot fewer voters for the Republicans by taking the white voters out. The southern and midwest states are blue for the majority parts. Those blue districts, typically red, are usually rural. On the other hand, it indicates that white voters are the key for the Republican party to win many districts that are originally republican. Many rural southern and midwest white voters are conservative.

What data is encoded in the visualization?

The encoded data are the projected results for the midterm election in 2018 without white voters. The data have details for whether each district is predicted to be democratic or republican.

How does the visualization encode the data?

IThe visualization encodes data through a map and colors. The map tells districts in each state, and colors are traditionally represented as democratic or republican.

What tasks do readers perform on the visualization?

Readers can compare the number of districts projected for each party, and they can also see where the districts are predicted to support each party, both without white voters. Voters can mouse over each district and see details.

How are the five principles applied to this visualization?

  • Beautiful: It utilizes the colors well, where blue and red are a contrast. The map has white boundaries between districts, which does not overwhelm the colors indicating parties.
  • Functional: This graph demonstrates each district, and people can easily mouse on any of them and see the details. The top tally also tells people which party has a projected majority without white voters.
  • Insightful: The data separate out a certain race and tell the voting patterns from then. People can compare this with their perceptions about voting and see how much white voters are influencing each district.
  • Truthful: The visualization is truthful because the data are from the registered voters’ information. It also conforms people’s expectations that non-white voters are more liberal compared to white voters.
  • Enlightening: People will find this visualization enlightening because it is a timely topic. The midterm election is coming in a few weeks, and it is also a critical election looking at a few important issues in the county. Understanding who will vote for which party helps understand the problems needed to solve.

Why is this visualization good?

This visualization is good because it investigates an important issue, which is the racial divide in voters. With the midterm election coming up, it helps people rethink the current political environment around this country and the reason behind the divide, such systemic racism and immigration. In addition, the visualization is clear with the map and the grid. The grid explicitly shows which party is dominating, and the map helps people identify where non white voters are voting for whom. Even though it is not creative about the colors, the traditional red and blue are easy for people to understand.
